Deleuze and Guattari's A Thousand Plateaus is structured around this concept. Basically, it's grass (or a hypertext). Anything for which any point can connect to any other point.

This contrasts what D+G call "arborescent" structures (another word for trees). These structures branch out from a common center like a genealogy or a hierarchy. Arborescence is the realm of taxonomies, categorization, and all-in-all Western-rational science. Rhizomes, on the other hand, are what we finds ourselves in currently: they are spaces of emergence, structured from the bottom-up, orderless, and traversible from any starting point.

The rhizome is the basic structure of knowledge in the Internet, and the basis for the Zettelkasten system; however, a Zettelkasten might be more of a "heterarchy," employing both elements of arborescence and rhizomes.
