She is a foil for S. Bartmann, a South-African woman brought to France and treated like an object; Bartmann's voice was often used to comment on French society. Duras uses a similar trope here, but treats it with much more realism and sensitivity, pointing out philosophical and ideological deficiencies in French society rather than the more superficial mockeries of Bartmann-imitators.
