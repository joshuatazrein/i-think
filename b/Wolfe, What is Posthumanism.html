Fundamentally, posthumanism stems from a resistance to totality & the binarism of Cartesian (and European metaphysical) logic. Transhumanism is not a sub-branch of posthumanism but a hyper-humanism which intensifies the rationalist & teleological goals of human perfection through technology.

posthumanism
  • xxxii posthumanism has connections to Romantic thought a la Emerson (i.e. concealment of the world)
  • xii originated at the Macy converences on cybernetics, then was picked up by Luhmann and Hayles; Haraway's "A Cyborg Manifesto" probably the best-known work
  • xiii now more thoughts tend to trend towards transhumanism (see posthumanism.com) xi
  • xv "[posthumanism] comes both before and after humanism": both reevaluating humanity as integrated, but also acknowledging the contemporary moment
  • xvii this doesn't mean that posthumanism can't dramatically inform disability theory and animality theory; in fact, it necessitates this actual justice
transformation of thought
  • xvi "the nature of thought itself must change if it is to be posthumanist" (see Heidegger, "Letter on Humanism")
  • xix Luhmann: 'reality ... is what one does not perceive when one perceives it'
  • xx-xxi mysticism of humanism: "pluralism = monad" a la Deleuze & Guattari; Derrida's "openness from closure"
  • xxiii we can no longer talk about "a" body Deleuze & Guattari, "How to Become a Body Without Organs"
  • xxiv first comes noise (Kristeva, Serres), then comes a subject
  • xxxiv noise, visuality, and sensoriums are deeply integrated into the network of this thought
  • xxvi for Derrida, language is the deepest prosthetic technology
critique of (trans)humanism
  • xi humanism = dignity and worth of all people
  • xi posthumanism.com has a definition which most posthumanists would oppose; in fact that definition (and transhumanism more specifically) constitutes more a hyper-humanism
  • xvi transhuman has recourse to the anthropological universals of posthumanism
  • xiv-xv "the fundamental anthropolical dogma associated with humanism [is that] 'the human' is achieved by escaping or repressing not just [humanity's] animal origins in nature, the biological, and the evolutionary, but more generally by tenscending the bonds of materiality and embodiment altogether"
  • xxi Luhmann: "The history of European rationality can be described as the history of the dissolution of a rationality continuum that had connected the observer in the world with the world" (see Heidegger) xvi
